Breakpoint

The day is bright and vibrant.
The hot sun hits my neck,
A bead of sweat rolls down onto my shirt.

“Damn,” I say, “It’s hot”.
“Should’ve brought a hat,” he says.
Every day, every hour, I spend with him. Practicing.
He makes me play,
And through the heat, I know that this,
This, is what it takes -to be great.

So, I walk, walk up to the base line.
Griping, squeezing, the fuzzy green ball in my hand.
I look up, the sun blinding my eyes.

“Again, the hat would block out that sun,” he shouts from across the clay court. Almost every day he says that.
I block him out,

He’s just noise.
They’re all just noise.

I look up again, squinting.
I toss the ball up,
High,
Higher,
Highest.
And then,
When it reaches it’s peak,
And begins to descend,
I hit it,
Slapping it down with my racket.

I look forward,
Trying to see what I made with that serve.

It looks perfect as it crosses my side,
about to go over the net,
And…
DINK!
It hits the top of the net,
And falls,
Falls down onto my side.

“Let!” He yells, hands cupped to his mouth.

I sigh.
I’ll have to do that again.

Gotta Crack an Egg to Make An Omlet

Tk- tk- tk,
FWOOSH!
The heat hits my face,
like a warm summer day.
And- with, a small stick of butter,
The pan begins to sizzle.

The sink is turned on,
Water pours onto my hand.
Water, still dripping- from my hand,
A flick, flick, flick-ing motion,
Onto the pan.
The grease jumps,
Up, from the skillet

The pan is ready.

The white egg,
white as snow,
It is cracked on the edge of the pan.
The fingers pry,
Pry the white oval thing,
Open so the gooey clear liquid pours out,
Along with a yolk,
So yellow, it could be,
hotter than the sun.

So the egg sizzles, sizzles,
And the goo slowly turns into a white,
Thing that is smooth.
Smooth, and slippery.

The thing solidifies on the bottom,
And the thing is flipped,
Flipped with a metal spatula.

The bottom is brown, a bit overcooked.
But it's perfect.
The imperfections are perfect.

A pinch of salt,
A dash of pepper,
And a quick flick of garlic.

The egg is rested on a plate,
Atop- a piece of toasted bread.

The egg, is done.

Oh flip, I dropped it.

Running The Wall

There I lay, dangling from the wall,
So close, but so far from the top
“There’s one to your left!” he called.
“No it's too far! I’ll fall!”
One wrong move, and I’ll flop.

My fingers cried in pain! If only I was tall,
Tall enough to reach the top, and then touch the belay lock.
Should I continue, or should I stall?
But there! I found it! A path to take! A hall!
No! It’s too far! I’ll have to stop.

And so, I had to fall;
fall because my fingers couldn’t lock.
They couldn’t lock onto the wall.
The silence screamed as I descended- it didn’t help me at all.
I had to wipe up my shame, wipe it up with a mop.

I can do it again, the wall!
Yes, I will do it again! And this time, I’ll reach the top.
I won’t fall!
I’ll show them all:
From stone to wall,
I can hop
